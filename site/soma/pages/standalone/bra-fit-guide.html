Soma’s Always Perfect Fit

Expert fit tips on getting Soma’s always perfect fit.

1 Straps

Straps should:

  • Stay in place and feel comfortable.
  • Provide additional lift to your bra.

You should:

  • Be able to slip a finger between your shoulder and strap.
  • Readjust your straps with every wearing.

Soma Fit Tip:

  • If your straps won't stay in place and have been tightened, try a bra with a ballet back, racerback or straps that can crisscross.
  • If straps are digging in to your shoulders, your bra is too small or you may need a style that provides better support.
  • A supportive band, and proper fitting cups, center front and underwire will alleviate shoulder discomfort.

2 Cups

Cups

Cups should be smooth and uplifting.

Proper fitting cups provide uplift and a smooth look under clothing.

  • The edges of the cups should lie flat against your body.
  • Your breasts should face forward and be positioned mid-way between your shoulders and elbows.

Soma Fit Tip:

  • If your cups gap or pucker, your cup size is either too small or you need a style with less coverage.
  • If you overflow or have bulging at the top or sides of the cups, your cups are too small or you need a style with more coverage.

3 Center Front

Center Front

The center front should be flat against your skin.

The center front adds support to the band.

  • The triangular section between the cups should lie flat against your body.

Soma Fit Tip:

If the center front is lifting off your skin, the cups are too small. Note: the exception for this is in some minimizer bras, which are specially designed to reduce the bustline and may not lie flat at the center front.

4 Underwire

Underwire

The underwire should encircle your breast.

Underwire adds support to the cups.

  • The underwire should fully encircle your breast from under the arm to the front of your chest.
  • The underwire should lay flat against your skin.

Soma Fit Tip:

If your underwire is uncomfortable, pokes, pinches or rests on your breast, the cups are too small.

5 Band

Band

The band should be level around your body.

The band provides the primary support in your bra.

  • The band should be level all the way around your body and positioned just below your shoulder blades.
  • You should be able to run a finger under the band.
  • The band should be snug, but comfortable when you are seated.

Soma Fit Tip:

If the band is riding up in the back and you can run more than two fingers under it, your band is too big. If the band feels tight when standing or sitting, your band is too small.
